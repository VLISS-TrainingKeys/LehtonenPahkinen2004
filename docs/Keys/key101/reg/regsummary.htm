NOTE. The known population total of UE91 is T=15098 persons. Use this figure as a reference when comparing the results from different samples.

Conclusions:

 

-     SRS estimates for the UE91 total vary considerably depending on the sample size and the sample configuration given the sample size. Regression estimated UE91 totals seem to be very stable compared to the SRS estimates. And regression estimates seem often to be quite close to the known total parameter T=15098.

 

-     SRS standard error estimates are very large compared to the standard error estimates of the regression estimator. Standard error estimates for the regression estimator tend to decrease with increasing the SRS sample size. This does not hold for the SRS estimator of the UE91 total. The reason is that a SRS estimate and its standard error estimate depend heavily on the presence or absence of the largest element (the largest value for UE91) in a given sample.

 

-     The observed large variation of the SRS estimates is caused by the large variance of the SRS estimator. And the small variation of the regression estimates is due to the small variance of the regression estimator. Thus, the accuracy of the regression estimator of the UE91 total is much better than that of the SRS estimator. Good accuracy is obtained in regression estimation because a very strong auxiliary variable (HOU85) is available.

 

-     More light on the relative behaviour of the estimators can be thrown when turning to Monte Carlo experiments. There, PPS sampling is examined in addition to regression estimation for a SRS sample.
