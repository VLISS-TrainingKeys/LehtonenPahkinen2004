INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 101b PART A: MONTE CARLO SIMULATIONS

INTRODUCTION

1) Simulations will be made by using the Province'91 population data as the frame population. Sampling frame will be shown from the link on the left.

2) Sample size in each simulated sample (10,100,500 or 1000 samples) is 8 (n=8).

3) The purpose of the simulations is to examine and compare the relative behavior of the HT estimator for SRSWOR, HT estimator for PPS and the REG estimator for SRSWOR.

NOTE! 

HT estimator for SRSWOR sample: No auxiliary information is used.

HT estimator for PPS sample: Auxiliary information (HOU85) is used in sampling phase by using PPS sampling. (Systematic or WOR-type sequential PPS)

REG estimator for SRSWOR sample: Auxiliary information (HOU85) is used in estimation phase for a SRSWOR sample by using regression estimation.

 

SIMULATING SAMPLES

1) Please select the number of samples you want to generate from the drop-down menu on the left.

2) Please examine the behaviours of the three estimators:

Mean of estimates. Compare with the known population value T=15098.

Bias properties and absolute relative bias (ARB) of estimates.

Standard error (s.e) and root mean squared error (ROOT MSE) of estimates.

3) Distribution of estimates is shown in the graphs.
