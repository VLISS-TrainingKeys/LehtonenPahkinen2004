INSTRUCTIONS FOR KEY 145: LINEARIZATION METHOD

In this part we will demonstrate how the estimation of the variance of a combined ratio type proportion or mean estimator can be carried out with the linearization method. The reason for the use of linearization is that the proportion and mean estimators should be treated here as nonlinear estimators of type y/x where both the numerator y and the denominator x are random variables. The denominator x is random because the sampling design of the MFH Survey is a stratified two-stage design where the cluster sample sizes are not fixed in advance, and the subgroup size is not fixed.

 HOW TO MANAGE THIS EXERCISE

In this exercise, variance approximations are calculated using the linearization method for the proportion estimate of the binary variable CHRON (Chronic morbidity; 1 present, 0 otherwise) and the mean estimate of the continuous variable SYSBP (Systolic blood pressure).

Please examine first the MFH Survey data set: Variables and the cluster sample sums of CHRON and PHYS.  You will note that the sampling design is a paired clusters design with 24 strata and two sample clusters drawn from each stratum.

Results of variance estimation by linearization are displayed in the final box of the output pages. The variable phat refers to CHRON and is the proportion estimate of chronic morbidity in 30-64 years old males. V_des_phat is the corresponding variance approximation.

The variable yhat refers to SYSBP and is the estimate of mean systolic blood pressure in 30-64 years old males. V_des_yhat is the corresponding variance approximation.
