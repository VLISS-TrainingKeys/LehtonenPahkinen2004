INSTRUCTIONS FOR KEY 250 PART A: Test of Independence by SUDAAN/CROSSTAB

In this part we will demonstrate how to carry out a test of independence of health hazards of work (PHYS) and psychic strain (PSYCH3)  in the OHC Survey setting. The results obtained from a design-based test of independence are compared to those obtained from a model-based test of independence. The design-based test statistic is calculated by the SAS callable SUDAAN procedure CROSSTAB.

 HOW TO MANAGE THIS EXERCISE

A hypothesis of independence is stated as H0: pjk = pj+p+k with j=1,2 and k=1,2,3, or, analogously, H0: p11 - p1+p+1= 0 and p12 - p1+p+2 = 0.

Table 2. Design-based Wald test results for table PHYS*PSYCH3 are viewed.

Table 3. Model-based Pearson Chi-Squared test results for table PHYS*PSYCH3 are viewed.

NOTE: Pay attention to the following. The Design-based Wald test provides a reasonable testing procedure for an independence hypothesis of PHYS and PSYCH3 (the results in Table 2). The model-based test of independence (Table 3) produces too large χ2-values and therefore too small p-values. This is because the clustering effect is not accounted for the model-based χ2-test contrary to the design-based Wald test.
