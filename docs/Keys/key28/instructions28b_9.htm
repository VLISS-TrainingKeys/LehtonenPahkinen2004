INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 28 PART B: SRS ESTIMATION WITH DIFFERENT SAMPLE SIZES

In this exercise we examine SRSWOR estimation of the total of UE91 (number of unemployed in the province) and LAB91 (size of labour force in the province) and of the ratio UE91/LAB91 by using different size SRSWOR samples. The purpose is to examine how sample size (n) affects to the total estimate and to the ratio estimate and their corresponding standard errors. We use Province'91 data set as the frame population. 

In the calculation we use the SAS procedure SURVEYMEANS. (Tested with SAS 9.1)

HOW TO MANAGE THIS EXERCISE

1) View sampling frame from the menu frame.

2) Select the sample size (n=4,8,16 or 32) of SRSWOR sample from the dropdown menu on the left and press submit to view the results of the SRSWOR estimation for the total of UE91 and LAB91 and for the ratio UE91/LAB91.

3) Examine the SRSWOR estimates for the total of UE91, LAB91 and for the ratio UE91/LAB91 and their standard errors estimated with different sample sizes. Pay also attention to the 95% confidence limits of the estimates.

4) View summary of results from the dropdown menu to examine how sample size affects to the SRSWOR estimates for the total and their standard errors.

 

After completing these steps please examine the SAS macro to perform such analysis for your own purposes.
