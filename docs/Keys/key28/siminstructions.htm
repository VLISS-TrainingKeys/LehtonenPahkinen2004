INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 28 PART C: MONTE CARLO SIMULATION TECHNIQUES

In this part we perform Monte Carlo simulations to examine the behaviour of the SRSWOR estimator of the total. Descriptive statistics of the Monte Carlo simulations will be displayed in numerical and in graphical format.

HOW TO MANAGE THIS EXERCISE

Select the sample size from the drop down menus on the left and press submit to view the results. The number of simulated samples in each simulation is 1000.

In each simulation, the population total, mean of estimates, standard error, bias, absolute relative bias (ARB) and root MSE (RMSE) are displayed for the estimator concerned. The distribution of estimates is viewed in the graph.

HOW TO INTERPRET THE RESULTS

The mean of the distribution of the estimates is expected to coincide closely with the corresponding population parameter. Here, the mean of the total estimates fits well with the corresponding parameter. Thus, in this respect the SRSWOR estimator works well. It is design unbiased.

On the other hand, if you take a closer examination to the histograms, you can see that in some of them there are two peaks. This cautions us against basing our inferences on an assumption of a normal distribution. This happens when the distribution of the study variable is skewed and the sample size is not large enough.Now, your task is to find what might be a SRSWOR sample size large enough when estimating each parameter and to decide whether SRSWOR is an appropriate technique for the estimation of the total of UE91 and LAB91 in the Province'91 population.
