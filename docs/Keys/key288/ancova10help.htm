KEY 288: Logistic ANCOVA

INTERPRETATION OF MODEL 1

MODEL 1

LOGIT (PSYCH) = SEX + AGE + PHYS + CHRON + SEX*AGE
+ SEX*PHYS + SEX*CHRON

The main goal of this exercise is to get familiar with the role of interaction terms in design-based logistic ANCOVA modelling. In addition to the intercept and main effect terms SEX, AGE, PHYS and CHRON, Model 1 includes all pair-wise interactions of SEX with the continuous predictors AGE, CHRON and PHYS: SEX*AGE, SEX*PHYS and SEX*CHRON. Please note the following points:

1. The examination of the p-values of the interaction terms in the estimated coefficients table shows that there is one interaction term that is significant at the 5 % level (the chosen reference level): SEX*AGE (p_beta = 0.0062 which is significant even at the 0.01 level). The other interactions are non-significant (for SEX*PHYS p_beta=0.1137 and for SEX*CHRON p_beta=0.2805). So these terms can be removed from the model without destroying the model fit. Thus, in addition to the intercept term, the final reduced model will include all the main effects SEX, AGE, PHYS and CHRON and the interaction term SEX*AGE.

2. The role of interaction terms in a model is demonstrated by the graphs. Fitted proportions of falling into the high psychic strain group are calculated for each model. For Model 1 (that includes all three interaction terms), the graphs show that the fitted proportions of falling into high psychic strain group vary from graph to graph. The graphs are constructed for each PHYS and CHRON combination (recall that PHYS=1 if physical health hazards are experienced, 0 otherwise, and CHRON=1 if a given person is chronically ill, 0 otherwise). Obviously, the variation in the levels of the lines is due to the inclusion of all main effects in the model, and the variation in directions of the lines is due to the inclusion of the three interactions.

3. Note that although graphs for Model 1 give an adequate description of the variation in the sample data set, all the variations cannot be generalized to the target population, this is because not all interaction terms are statistically significant. Graphs showing variations that can be generalized statistically will be obtained by constructing the final reduced model.

4. The final reduced model will be obtained from Model 1 by removing the following interaction effects: SEX*PHYS and SEX*CHRON (since they are not statistically significant at the reference level of 5 %).
