KEY 288: Logistic ANCOVA

INTERPRETATION OF MODEL 2

MODEL 2

logit(PSYCH) = SEX + AGE + PHYS + CHRON + SEX*AGE

In addition to the intercept and main effect terms SEX, AGE, PHYS and CHRON, Model 2 includes the only statistically significant (at the reference 5 % level) interaction term, that is, the term SEX*AGE. Please note the following points:

1. The examination of the p-value of the interaction term in the estimated coefficients table shows that SEX*AGE is significant at the 5 % level (the chosen reference level): for SEX*AGE p_beta = 0.0111. Thus, in addition to the intercept term, the final reduced model includes all the main effects SEX, AGE, PHYS and CHRON and the interaction term SEX*AGE.

2. The role of the interaction term SEX*AGE in Model 2 is demonstrated by similar graphs as for Model 1. Fitted proportions of falling into the high psychic strain group are calculated for Model 2. The graphs show that for Model 2, fitted proportions of falling into upper psychic strain group vary from graph to graph much less than for Model 1. Again, the variation in the levels of the lines is due to the inclusion of all the main effects in the model. Less variation in directions of the lines is due to the inclusion of only one interaction term. Note that for males, the lines are parallel in all graphs. This holds for females as well.

3. Note that graphs for Model 2 give a description of the variation that can be statistically generalized to the target population from where the survey data has been collected. Graphs indicate (perhaps surprisingly) that for males, prevalence of high psychic strain increases with increasing age, and for females, prevalence decreases. Further, at a given age, the risk of belonging to the upper strain group is larger for chronically ill people and for people experiencing physical health hazards at work. The level is highest when both risk factors are present. This holds for both males and females. However, for males, the levels tend to be lower than for females, for all ages.

4. Odds Ratio (OR) statistics show that sex-age adjusted OR is 1.76 for CHRON and 1.32 for PHYS.
