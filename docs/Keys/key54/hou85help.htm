Training key 59: The role of auxiliary information in PPS sampling

INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS WHEN HOU85 IS USED AS THE SIZE MEASURE VARIABLE

 

The main goal in this exercise is to show the role of auxiliary information in PPS sampling. In principle, any of the continuous variables in the frame population could be used as a size measure variable. However, it is important to examine whether they are suitable auxiliary variables before applying them. Please pay attention to the following points in the output:

1) Correlation between UE91 and HOU85:

The correlation of UE91 and HOU85 is ρ=0.997. Strong positive correlation is one indicator of a good size measurement variable. In the sense of strong correlation, HOU85 appears to be a very suitable size measurement variable. However, strong correlation itself is not sufficient enough for efficient estimation in a PPS sample.

2) Scatterplot of UE91 against HOU85:

A linear trend is observed from the scatterplot of UE91 against HOU85. This is a first good sign. The other good sign is that when UE91 approaches zero, HOU85 approaches zero as well. Thus, the regression model UE91=β*HOU85 holds approximately. These are all indicators of a suitable auxiliary variable.

HOU85 provides us a sufficient auxiliary variable and efficient estimation in a PPS sample. As we may note from the Monte Carlo simulation study, PPSWOR estimator is approximately unbiased with very small standard error providing efficient estimation in a PPS sample. This is due to the good auxiliary variable.
