INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 54: Estimation under systematic PPS sampling

In this part we will examine how to perform the estimation under systematic PPS sampling. A sample of eight (n=8) municipalities is drawn with systematic PPS sampling from the Province'91 population such that the number of households (HOU85) is used as the size measure z.

In Province'91 population the cumulative sum of HOU85 over the population is 91753 and under systematic PPS sampling the sampling interval would be q=91753/8=11469. However, the largest element 'Jyvaskyla' has the value 26881 for the variable, which is more than twice the sampling interval. Therefore, the element would be drawn twice and the remaining 6 elements would be drawn from the remaining population elements. In this case, therefore, we first take the largest element 'Jyväskylä' with certainty and then draw 7 elements from the remaining population elements by systematic PPS sampling. Such a situation is commonly managed in the way described above.
