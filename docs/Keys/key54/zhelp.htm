Training key 59: The role of auxiliary information in PPS sampling

INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS WHEN Z IS USED AS A SIZE MEASURE VARIABLE

 

Z is an artificially created variable for pedagogical purposes. Variable Z has the mean 500 with variance 150. While variable Z is used as the size measurement variable, some problems occur. Please pay attention to the following points in the output:

1) Correlation between UE91 and Z:

The correlation of UE91 and Z is ρ= -0.008. Strong positive correlation is one indicator of a good size measurement variable. However, Z doesn't correlate with UE91 at all. In the sense of strong correlation, Z appears to be a weak size measurement variable.

2) Scatterplot of UE91 against Z:

There is no linear trend observed from the scatterplot of UE91 against Z. This is due to the correlation which is close to zero.  This means that the regression UE91=α + β*Z does not hold well. This is also an indicator of a non-suitable auxiliary variable.

By performing the Monte Carlo simulation study, we note that although PPSWOR estimator is approximately unbiased, standard error is very large. By using non-suitable size measurement variable, such as Z, the situation may become even worse than with a SRSWOR estimator. Z is not a sufficient auxiliary variable for efficient estimation in a PPS sample in this case study.
