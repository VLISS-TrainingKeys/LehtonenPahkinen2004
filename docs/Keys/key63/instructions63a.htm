INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 63 PART A: CALCULATION OF THE PARAMETER DEFF FOR STRATIFIED SIMPLE RANDOM SAMPLING WITH PROPORTIONAL ALLOCATION

In this exercise we calculate the design effect DEFF under stratified simple random sampling with proportional allocation. We use the Province'91 data set as the frame population. The total T of the study variable UE91 (number of unemployed in the province) is to be estimated. Results of Example 3.1 will be reproduced.

The population (N=32) is divided into two subpopulations, one for urban municipalities (N1=7) and the other for rural municipalities (N2=25). Proportional allocation is the simplest allocation scheme and widely used in practice. The number of sample elements nh in stratum h is given by nh,pro = n * Nh/N = n * Wh, where Wh=Nh/N is the stratum weight. The proportional allocation scheme produces sample sizes n1=2 and n2=6 for the total of eight sampling elements (n=8).

The DEFF of t_hat is given with analysis of variance by equation (3.4):
DEFFstr,pro(t_hat) = (within-stratum variance)/(total variance) = MSW / S2 = 0.79.
