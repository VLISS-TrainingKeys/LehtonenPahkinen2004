INSTRUCTIONS FOR TRAINING KEY 63 PART B: DIFFERENT ALLOCATION SCHEMES UNDER STRATIFIED SIMPLE RANDOM SAMPLING

We use the Province'91 data set as the frame population to examine the different allocation schemes. The population is divided into two strata, one for urban municipalities and the other for rural municipalities. Stratum size for urban municipalities is N1=7 and for rural municipalities N2=25. A stratified simple random sample of eight (n=8) municipalities is drawn, and the appropriate stratum sample sizes are calculated under proportional, optimal and power allocation schemes. The total of the study variable UE91 (number of unemployed in the province) is to be estimated. Results of Example 3.2 will be reproduced.

In proportional allocation the number of sample elements nh in stratum h is given by
nh,pro = n * Nh / N = n * Wh, where Wh=Nh / N is the stratum weight. It presupposes knowledge of the stratum sizes only, since the sampling fraction nh / Nh is constant for each stratum. Proportional allocation guarantees an equal share of the sample in all the strata, but can produce less efficient estimates than sometimes expected. Here, proportional allocation produces sample sizes n1=2 and n2=6 for the total of eight sampling elements (n=8).

Optimal allocation can be used if Sh, the standard deviations for individual strata of the study variable, are known. The number of sample units in stratum h is given by
nh,opt = n * ( Nh * Sh ) / ( ∑ Nh * Sh ). This type of allocation provides the most efficient estimates under stratified sampling. Here, optimal allocation produces sample sizes n1=4 and n2=4.

Power allocation is suggested for surveys in which there are numerous small strata and that there is a need for precise estimates at each stratum level. The number of sample units in stratum h is given by
nh,pow = n * [ (Thz)a C.Vhy ] / [ ∑ (Thz)a C.Vhy ], where Thz is the stratum total of an auxiliary variable z and C.Vhy is the coefficient of variation of y in stratum h. The constant a is called the power of allocation. In this exercise a=0. Power allocation (approximate) produces sample sizes n1=4 and n2=4 and power allocation (exact) with stratum specific coefficient ch produces sample sizes n1=3 and n2=5.
